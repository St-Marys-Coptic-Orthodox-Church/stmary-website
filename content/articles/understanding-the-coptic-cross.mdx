The Coptic cross is one of the oldest Christian symbols in existence. Unlike the Latin cross most commonly seen in the West, the Coptic cross features equal arms and a circular nimbus, symbolizing eternity and the resurrection.

Its distinctive form connects modern Coptic Christians directly to the first-century church established by St. Mark the Evangelist in Alexandria.
